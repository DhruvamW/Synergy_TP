# Task 8 – Data Analysis and Statistics for Domain Data

## Overview

This task focuses on the theoretical foundations of data analysis and descriptive statistics used in science and engineering. Instead of implementing code, the objective is to understand how numerical data should be interpreted, summarized, and visualized before any meaningful analysis or machine learning is performed.

The report emphasizes that statistics alone are not sufficient for drawing conclusions. Every measurement must be interpreted using its variable name, unit of measurement, experimental conditions, and domain knowledge.

---

## Objectives

- Understand the purpose of descriptive statistics.
- Learn how measures of center and spread describe a dataset.
- Interpret data distributions and the effect of sample size.
- Understand the role of outliers and when they should be investigated.
- Learn how to select appropriate plots for different types of data.
- Differentiate between correlation and causation.
- Interpret measurements correctly across different engineering and scientific domains.
- Appreciate the importance of context in statistical analysis.

These examples demonstrate how the same statistical methods are applied differently depending on the domain and experimental conditions.

---

## Key Learning Outcomes

After completing this task, the following concepts were understood:

- Statistics summarize data but do not explain why patterns exist.
- The mean can be misleading when outliers are present.
- The median and IQR are more reliable for skewed datasets.
- Sample size strongly affects the reliability of statistical conclusions.
- Outliers should be investigated rather than removed automatically.
- Different plots serve different analytical purposes.
- Correlation does not imply causation.
- Measurements from different domains cannot be compared unless they describe the same quantity under similar conditions.
- Every numerical value is meaningful only when accompanied by its variable name, unit, and experimental context.
